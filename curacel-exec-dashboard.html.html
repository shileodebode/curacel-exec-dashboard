Risks & Gaps
Prioritised issues that require leadership attention or operational decisions.
🔴 Critical — Needs immediate action
🔴
Finance Expense Approval workflow is down
n8n #21 has been inactive with no backup owner. Victoria is the sole owner. Weekly reimbursements are manual. This is the highest-urgency fix — it is simple to reactivate and the business impact is immediate.
🔴
No SLA framework exists for any live agent
There is no defined uptime standard, no output quality review process, and no escalation path when an agent fails or degrades. The 10 live agents are running without formal performance accountability.
🔴
Automated QA & Testing agent is overdue with no update
Was due January 31. Now overdue by over 3 months with no documented blocker and no revised timeline. Engineering has no automated QA coverage as a result.
🟡 High — Address within 30 days
⚠️
14 workflows have no backup owner
If any single owner goes on leave or leaves the company, 14 automated processes break with no recovery plan. Includes all of Pelumi's recruitment flows, Precious's 1:1 reminders, Sophie's claims quality workflow, and all 4 of Joseph and Tracy's CS workflows.
⚠️
Claims Browsing Agent — actual status unknown
Listed as "in development" with Fikayo as builder, but the audit note reads "may not have been coded." This agent appears on the roadmap and in stakeholder communications but may not exist. Needs immediate verification before it is referenced further.
⚠️
Samuel Bamgbola is a single point of failure for AI Ops delivery
Samuel owns 6 of the 10 live agents. If he becomes unavailable, a significant portion of AI Ops production capacity is lost. The team needs a second builder at his level, or more structured knowledge transfer.
⚠️
5 People Ops workflows are inactive with no decision made
Xlabs, Exec 2.0, Senior PM (old), PeopleOps (Eng-built), and Pulse Check are all inactive. No record of why they were stopped or whether they should be revived. These create clutter and ambiguity in the workflow inventory.
🔵 Strategic — Worth leadership visibility
💡
Finance has no AI agent — the largest gap by department
Finance is the only department with zero live agents and a broken automation. The LLM Financial Reporting Bot has a repository but has not been developed. This is a high-value gap given the reporting burden on the finance team.
💡
No centralised AI Ops command centre (Curvis)
There is no single place to view the health, output, and status of all agents. Monitoring is ad hoc. As the agent count grows, this becomes increasingly untenable. Curvis — the proposed AI Ops Command Centre — addresses this directly.
💡
AI adoption is not being measured
There is no usage tracking, no quality feedback loop, and no way to demonstrate ROI to leadership. Without measurement, it is impossible to know whether agents are being used as intended or delivering value.
